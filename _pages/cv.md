Work experience

* Aug 2024 - Apr 2025: Master's Thesis Project - A constrained extended Lagrangian approach for computing electrostatic forces
  * EPFL Switzerland, On-Site
  * With the MARVEL Inspire fellowship, I worked at EPFL for my Master's thesis project. My thesis with Prof. Sara Bonella at CECAM aims to efficiently implement a novel method for simulating electrostatic interactions of charged particles in molecular dynamics. The algorithm leverages a constrained extended Lagrangian formalism to integrate the Poisson equation, promising computational advantages in highly parallelized setups. A liquid molten salt serves as the test system. The study covers the theoretical framework, system setup, and parallelization strategies used to accelerate simulations within an in-house code, with plans to convert the code to a library in order to publish it for public use.
  * Supervisor: Prof. Sara Bonella
  * Publication submitted.

* May - Aug, 2024: Internship - Star Formation through Magnetohydrodynamics 
  * The Australian National University, Canberra, On-Site
  * I worked under the guidance of Prof. Christoph Feddarath at the Australian National University, Canberra, Australia, from May 2024 to August 2024, with the FRT Scholarship. I completed a research project in Star Formation wherein I studied the influence of optical thickness on turbulent star-forming molecular clouds. My work was an extension of Prof. Christoph's paper in 2021 where the same work was done in an optically thin environment. The results fortify the understanding that optical thickness in clouds plays a significant role in the way clouds collapse and that optical thickness cannot be ignored.
  * Supervisor: Prof. Christoph Federrath 
  * Publication submitted to MNRAS

* May - Aug 2023: Internship - Planet Formation through Pebble Accretion
  * Max Planck Institute for Solar System Research, Goettingen, On-Site
  * I worked under the guidance of Dr Joanna Drążkowska at the Max Planck Institute for Solar System Research in Göttingen, Germany, from May 2023 to August 2023, with a DAAD-WISE Scholarship. I completed a research project on Planet Formation wherein I studied the influence of disk turbulence, pebble fragmentation and stellar metallicity on planet growth by pebble accretion model using the pebble predictor (pioneered by Dr Drążkowska) along with the analytical fit from Ormel & Liu (2018); Liu & Ormel (2018). The results fortify the theory that the likelihood of planet formation (for close-in planets) is high when there is high pebble fragmentation, low disk turbulence, or even a very metallic star.
  * Supervisor: Dr Joanna Drążkowska  
  * Published in MNRAS

* May - Aug 2021: Reading Project - Trajectory of Light around a Black Hole
